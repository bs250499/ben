Police Acronyms

1.      POLSA - a specially-trained officer who advised on the best approach to carry out searches

 

-Police Search Advisor

 

2.      FIM - Inspector rank officer who oversees all the live critical incidents going on in the area

 

-Force Incident Manager

 

3.      NFA - when police cannot obtain enough evidence for a charge and the case is dropped

 

-No Further Action

 

4.      CHIS - known as a 'grass' or 'snitch' by those on the wrong side of the law

 

-Covert Human Intelligence Source

 

5.      D&D - not Dungeons & Dragons, mostly involves imbibing too much and causing fights in public

 

-Drunk & Disorderly

 

6.      FLO - specially-trained officers who work closely with victims of serious crimes and/or their families

 

-Family Liaison Officer

 

7.      ANPR - Camera computerised system that allows identification of vehicles and track their movements

 

-Automatic Number Plate Recognition

 

8.      BWV - recording equipment worn by an officer to record footage of an incident

 

-Body-worn Video

 

9.      OCG - three or more people who agree to act together to carry out criminal activities on a regular basis

 

-Organised Crime Group

 

10.   SOCO - thanks to American TV, also known as CSI

 

-Scenes of Crime Officer

True or False

1.      It is impossible to hum while holding your nose.

 

-True

 

2.      Sailors in the Royal Navy need special permission to grow a beard.

 

-True

 

3.      You are more likely to have a weird or scary dream while sleeping on your back.

 

-False - more likely when sleeping on the stomach

 

4.      The most widely printed book in the world is the Bible.

 

-False - the IKEA catalogue has over 200 million copies circulated annually

 

5.      Kummerspeck the German for the weight gained during emotional eating.

 

-True - loosely translates as 'grief bacon'

 

6.      The world's largest pyramid is found in Egypt.

 

-False - The Great Pyramid of Cholula in Mexico holds the record for the largest pyramid in the world, with a base four times larger than Giza's and almost double the volume

 

7.      Dolphins have names.

 

-True - research has found that dolphins use their unique vocal whistles to identify and differentiate one another

 

8.      Apple seeds contain arsenic.

 

-False - but they do contain cyanide

 

9.      A single strand of spaghetti is referred to as spaghetto.

 

-True - spaghetti is a plural word in Italian. Who is having just one strand?

 

10.   Switzerland prohibits the ownership of just one guinea pig.

 

-True - as guinea pigs are social animals, having only one is considered animal abuse
